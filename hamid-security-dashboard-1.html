Cyber Awareness

🎣
PHISHING ATTACKS

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals disguise themselves as trusted entities to steal your sensitive data via fake emails, websites, or messages.

  • Always verify the sender's email address
  • Never click suspicious links
  • Check URLs before entering credentials
  • Enable email filtering systems
🔑
STRONG PASSWORDS

Weak passwords are the #1 entry point for hackers. A strong password is your first and most critical line of defense against unauthorized access.

  • Use 12+ characters minimum
  • Mix UPPER, lower, numbers, symbols
  • Never reuse passwords
  • Use a password manager
🔐
TWO-FACTOR AUTH

2FA adds a second layer of protection. Even if your password is stolen, attackers cannot access your account without the second verification step.

  • Enable 2FA on all critical accounts
  • Use authenticator apps over SMS
  • Backup your 2FA recovery codes
🦠
MALWARE & RANSOMWARE

Malicious software can encrypt your files, steal data, or give attackers full control of your system. Prevention is far better than recovery.

  • Keep software always updated
  • Never download from unknown sources
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Regular offline backups
📡
PUBLIC Wi-Fi RISKS

Public networks are hunting grounds for attackers running man-in-the-middle attacks. Your data travels unencrypted and can be intercepted.

  • Always use a VPN on public Wi-Fi
  • Avoid banking on public networks
  • Disable auto-connect to networks
🕵️
SOCIAL ENGINEERING

Hackers manipulate human psychology to gain access. They impersonate authority figures, create urgency, and exploit trust to extract sensitive information.

  • Verify identities before sharing info
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests
  • Train your team regularly
